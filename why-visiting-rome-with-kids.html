    Rome with Kids: Tips and Info for a family vacation in the Eternal City

    By our Team of Authors

    Tips and Notes 8 mins read
    • Share this:

    The eternal city has got so many attractions to visit that making a choice is never easy, especially if you have kids with large years gaps. Most people that come to Rome want so see its historic attractions and art treasures and this can be suitable only to children aged 6 and above. The little ones cannot appreciate. Furthermore, carrying a stroller through the cobbles-paved streets of the city is not a pleasant experience.

    What are the options for very young kids? There are two big monumental parks in Rome worth visiting from different points of view: Villa Borghese and Villa Pamphili. Both are very close to the city center and scattered with centuries old trees and interesting monuments. You might go there with part of your family while the other one faces the crowds of the famous museums and archaeological sites.

    Another thing very young kids would love is to play with fountains. From Trevi to Navona there is plenty of water jets in the city center and even more astonishing, most of them are drinkable! What is better for you family in the hot summer days than to explore these big and small fountains often sided by an artistic wonder where you can take also great pictures.

    Going back to the older children they would enjoy a fun and educational visit of any museum or historic site starting of course from the Vatican Museums and the Colosseum. Entry tickets are often sold out last minute so better you plan tour visit in advance.

    There are nowadays many companies and guides that offer kid-friendly activities in those sites. Check their reviews and book one of more of their tours but be careful: two tours in one day is too much for the kids to concentrate end enjoy the most. It is preferable you take one each day and leave the rest of the day to more relaxed and playful activities.

    Terraces Great Panoramas

    Something that both older kids and parents would very much like is to enjoy the panorama from the most breathtaking terraces in Rome. There is plenty of choice. Here is a list of the top 4:

    - Janiculum Hill Terrace
    - Capitol Museum Terrace
    - Vittoriano Terrace
    - Pincio Promenade and Terrace

    Top Things to Do and Visit

    Here is a list instead of must see attractions in order of priority for a standard three days stay in Rome:

    - Vatican Museums, Sistine Chapel and Basilica of Saint Peter (half day)
    - Colosseum, Forums Vittoriano and Capitol Hill (3 hours)
    - Trevi Fountain, Spanish Steps, Piazza Navona and Pantheon (3 hours)
    - Castel Sant Angelo, Santa Maria Maggiore, St Clemens Undergrounds
    - Trastevere neighborough, Tiberine Island, Forum Boarium and Circus Maximus (half day)
    - Day Trip to Ostia Antica or Tivoli to visit some of the best preserved Ancient archeological sites (full day) - Appian Way Bicycle Ride (you can rent the bikes in many spots in Rome) (half day)

    Be Part of Our Online Community

    Get the latest tips and ask outher moms in our online community! It is free and you get access to all the info you need for your family travel planning.

    Last but not least, take all your family to one of the farmers morning markets where they can learn local colorful fruits vegetables and food specialties and taste them. Taste memories sometimes can last longer than images as Marcel Proust taught us.

    Alessia Ferrario and Giulia Occhini
